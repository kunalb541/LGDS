Dear Editor,

Please consider the enclosed manuscript, "Coherence, Coverability, and
Regret for Finite-Rank Projections of PSD Families," for publication in
Linear Algebra and its Applications.

The paper studies common optimality of a single rank-r orthogonal
projection across a finite family of positive semidefinite operators
under two natural scoring rules: the trace (Ky Fan) score tr(PM) and
the operator-norm score lambda_max(PKP). The main results are:

  - Existence of a common trace-score optimum is equivalent to
    r-coherence: all M_t share a common dominant r-dimensional
    invariant subspace.

  - Existence of a common operator-norm optimum is equivalent to
    r-coverability: a single rank-r subspace meets the leading
    eigenspace of every K_t nontrivially.

  - r-coherence implies r-coverability strictly; a 3x3 witness
    isolates the algebraic gap (the "fork").

  - When coverability fails, an eigenvalue-weighted surrogate B_lambda
    converts the non-Ky-Fan operator-norm objective into a Ky Fan
    eigenproblem, with sandwich and instance-dependent regret bounds
    that are tight in three structural regimes (rank-1, r-coverable,
    common-shape).

The variational ingredients are classical (Ky Fan trace maximisation
and the Rayleigh quotient characterisation). The contribution is to
place the two common-optimality laws side by side, prove the strict
fork between them, and develop the surrogate regret analysis.

The paper connects naturally with the common principal components
literature (Flury 1984, Krzanowski 1979, Pham 2001): r-coherence is a
common-principal-subspace condition strictly weaker than simultaneous
diagonalisation, and the operator-norm side opens a complementary
hitting-problem perspective on PSD families.

The manuscript is approximately 13 pages, theorem-driven, and includes
three numerical illustrations (one stationary linear Gaussian dynamical
system instance and two analytically exact 3x3 examples). The
reproduction code is openly available at https://github.com/kunalb541/LGDS.

This manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere. No external
funding was received for this work, and the author declares no
competing interests.
