For a finite family of positive semidefinite (PSD) operators on R^d
and a fixed rank r, this paper asks when a single rank-r orthogonal
projection is simultaneously optimal for every operator under two
natural scoring rules: the trace score tr(PM_t) (Ky Fan), and the
operator-norm score lambda_max(P K_t P). Existence of a common
optimal projection under the trace score is equivalent to r-coherence:
all M_t share a common dominant r-dimensional invariant subspace.
Existence of a common optimal projection under the operator-norm score
is equivalent to r-coverability: a single rank-r subspace nontrivially
intersects the leading eigenspace of every K_t. r-coherence implies
r-coverability but the converse fails; a 3x3 witness exhibits the
strict gap (the "fork"). When coverability fails for a finite-support
regime {A_i} of local PSD operators, the eigenvalue-weighted surrogate
B_lambda = sum_i w_i lambda_1(A_i) u_i u_i^T (with u_i the leading
eigenvector of A_i) provides a Ky-Fan-tractable approximation of the
regime-averaged operator-norm objective; we prove a sandwich
F_tilde(P) <= F(P) <= F_tilde(P) + sum_i w_i lambda_2(A_i) and an
instance-dependent regret bound F(P_F*) - F(P_B*) <= sum_i
w_i lambda_2(A_i)(1 - ||P_F* u_i||^2) tight in three regimes (rank-1
A_i, r-coverable case, common-shape family). Numerical illustrations
include a stationary linear Gaussian dynamical system certifying
non-coherence, the 3x3 fork witness, and a 3x3 surrogate counterexample
in which the coarse regret bound overestimates the true regret by a
factor of 36.
